I have to get that done within the next few days (I should have gotten part of it done today.) Tomorrow then, I guess. And articles to write. Newspaper will once again force me to write articles of garbage. So, that's a few more page of text to write. Tests. A lot of those to remember. A physics test tomorrow that I'd prefer to study for (but, obviously didn't prefer enough).

At the lecture on Thursday, Amy Tan spoke about how she took so many classes in college that she had to read and write a paper on a book once a day. Thinking about how other people can do these amazing feats of concentrated effort is both disheartening and inspiring.

Here I am, struggling to write a paper in two weeks. Pathetic.

And other things continue to harass me, chiefly being the Lego League program at the nearby elementary school. Our robotics team is not to a level where we can competently lead elementary school children in a technical setting. They yell, fight, cry, distract, play, all sorts of things that children are supposed to do. It makes building a robot... difficult. The "research project" that they need to do is very much lacking as well. I don't even think a distinct topic has even been chosen yet. It's a very stressful situation.

Tomorrow brings surprises.

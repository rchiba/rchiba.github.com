Today, I got even feistier with Blender. I made three models, spent about 6 hours modeling, and am trying to create an industrial landscape/building similar to the petroleum tank above.

But, the really odd thing is, this program has gotten to my head. I find myself trying to control reality using the hotkeys, trying to navigate through 3d space using the middle mouse button, shift, and the numeric keypad. I look at a building and see not a building, but see the building as planes made up of vertices and textures, the curves being simulated by computer algorithms. To move around, I try to hold down the shift key and drag, but find my hands clicking buttons that aren't there, my eyes staring into a monitor that does not exist.

And after using the program for so long, it's hard to draw a line between simulated reality, and reality itself. What I see on the computer is just as realistic to me as what I see outside of the computer. I'm slowly adjusting to handling 2 worlds at once, but it's sometimes confusing. The hotkeys for movement, edge selection, and scaling is so wired into my brain.

But, I think it's all worth it. Discovering Blender was and is like discovering the pen and pencil all over again. It's a new tool, a fun new tool to build a world out of nothing, and make it look good too.

So, that's the end of the fabled "Thanksgiving Weekend".

Now it's time to hit ground running, so to speak, and go to school tomorrow. Yippee!

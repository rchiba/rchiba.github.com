I come home at 7p.m. if I'm lucky, and I might have 30 minutes of spare time if I'm luckier. Usually I have to stay up just to do my homework or study for a test. It is very frustrating to me. I cannot smile, and any joy at all only lasts for a few seconds, then it melts away into anger, shame, sadness, depression, absentmindedness... etc. I can't concentrate, I can't comprehend, I have problems processing large batches of information, I am slower at math, I can't eat, and I can't be happy. What is happy anyways? Who needs happy when you're hopelessly overworked and despise your existance?

Math class frustrated me today because all of the younger children worked faster and more efficiently than I ever will. I did math at half their speed. I hate myself sometimes. Everyone seems to be better than me at everything. Reading, math, trivia, reflexes, and even playing the clarinet. I can't play my clarinet as good as anyone else. I'm frustrating myself and blaming everyone else for it, and I know, but it's my way of coping with my stress, and as Rachel Cheezedawg always says, "Don't rain on my parade, mister!"

I can't be happy. What is happy?

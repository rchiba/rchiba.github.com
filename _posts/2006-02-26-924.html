At this notion, I am astonished and speechless. Currently, I don't know how to react. On one hand, such an early arrival home means an increase in leisure activities and a decrease in homework stress. On the other hand, the abrupt change in my schedule means that I won't have the opportunity to socialize with my robotics comrades and screw around with the computers in the back, not to mention not having anymore excuses for being an irritable jerk.

When the pros and cons are weighed, I'll choose the bus any day.

This weekend, I rate two thumbs up. I was able to get back into the few hobbies that I cling to. To do something new, I decided to rig a character in Blender and learn how to use the Non-Linear Action (NLA) system. The NLA system is actually a fancy term for organizing actions into blocks. Movements in 3D computer animation are recorded as keyframes, or distinct poses in which bones of a skeleton are positioned in certain points in an XYZ graph. Being the fearful, cautious person I am, I decided to try doing a simple walk cycle. A walk-cycle involves many keyframes. What the NLA system does is store all of the walk-cycle keyframes as one block action. When someone thinks of walking, one thinks of the single action of walking, not a series of interconnected poses. In theory, this system makes actions (like walking, waving a hand, jumping) much easier to manage.

Or so they say. I spent two hours trying to find out how to get more than one block action to take place. In the end, I found out that I had to click a little button that was visible to me the whole time. Frustration combined with victory was never so sweet.

--- layout: post title: '' tags: - Blast From the Past status: private type: post published: false meta: blogger_blog: ryochiba.blogspot.com blogger_author: Ryohttp://www.blogger.com/profile/08925049298023370483noreply@blogger.com blogger_permalink: /2005/09/i-was-watching-news-and-cnn-was.html --- I was watching the news, and CNN was showing a supposedly "compelling" attempt to rescue a displaced dolphin in the aftermath of hurricane Katrina.

I agree with the newscasters. This report is "compelling".

Although, I find this "compelling" should be used as a negative connotation. To see about ten people, coming together, supplying countless hours, a large truck, and medical equipment to a dolphin came across to me as completely absurd.

I don't know about you, but I think it's logical to save the people before the animal. Why didn't these heartless animal helpers decide to help some humans for a chance? There are people dying out there, needing trucks, equipment, and effort. And to see people wasting these precious resources on a mere animal is shocking. It's disgusting how inhumane these people are being to their own kind. They make a statement in their "valiant rescue". They are telling me that the stupid, insignificant dolphin is more deserving of life than a human. I think these animal rescue "heroes" are shams. How can anyone value the life of an animal more than that of a human? It just goes without saying.

I suppose it's one of those irrational things that seem to never stop going on in the world. But, this time, it costs lives.

And that isn't good. If you're going to save a dolphin, stop and go save some people. People have potential to improve society. Dolphins have potential to reproduce, do circus tricks at over-priced theme parks, and die.

At Jefferson, I worked five hours on the actual animation. All scenes are complete-ish. Now, it's just the credits that have to be done. Then rendering. Then compositing (oh, the joy of compressing your dear child into a pixellated pile of poop.) All this work for a mediocre, 30-second animation.

But, I think it's important, this whole being an animator for the robotics team. Certainly, I wouldn't have learned this much CSS in a day if it wasn't for being on the Animation team.

Soon, I will need to come up with newspaper article ideas. The problem is, the things I enjoy reading about aren't what the school wants to read about. It's tough coming up with ideas. I think being busy has eased the pain. You can't feel from a frostbitten hand.

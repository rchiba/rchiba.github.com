And nothing to look forward to with hope,
So now and never any different. - The Death of the Hired Man by Robert Frost

I am a hypocrite.

I wish I didn't care about my GPA. I wish I could really believe that it's just a number. To not lie to myself. To believe that a student's actions are more important than the number the teacher stamps on their forehead. Maybe it has something to do with my identity. It's an identity that manifests itself in everyone I talk to. It's constantly in my thought. It's how I act. It's what I try to be. It's what other people thing I'm supposed to be. It's why I envy. Why I lust for goals I'll never ever reach.

Due to circumstances, I will soon be parting from the wonderful 4.0. The thing I've lied to you about for so long. In reality, I put a lot of value on it. When I lose it, my identity will no longer be as it used to be. Who am I? Am I being arrogant in harboring such feelings?

Is it worth making a scene about? Absolutely not. Or maybe I'm trying to justify my failure. The failure, it's a result of what I did, what I didn't, what I should've, what just wasn't. The loss is my fault. My stupid, arrogant, foolish, fault. I didn't solve the problems I needed to solve. Time was up. Papers collected. Bam. So, that's how it went. And that's how I feel about it. No one cares.

Didn't want to talk all day. Just felt angry and sick. So ashamed. Mad. Wanted to forget, wanted to believe in something. Wanted to be a good at something. A talent? Specialty? Passion? Everyone's special, right? People can create art, people are good at reading, people can sell used cars. Me?

Nothing. I have access to many great things. I'd love to keep them, for them to become part of me. To sketch out an identity for myself.

But, at the end of the day, on the inside, I have nothing to call my own. Everything's been loaned.

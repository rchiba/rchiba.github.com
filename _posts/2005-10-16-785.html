--- layout: post title: '' tags: - Blast From the Past status: private type: post published: false meta: blogger_blog: ryochiba.blogspot.com blogger_author: Ryohttp://www.blogger.com/profile/08925049298023370483noreply@blogger.com blogger_permalink: /2005/10/on-second-life-today-i-heard-from.html --- On Second Life today, I heard from an aquaintance about "NaNoWriMo".

I've heard of this mysterious "NaNoWriMo" in forums and chat gatherings all over, but, to tell you the truth, I never really understood what it was. I just recognized it.

That's why when I learned about what it actually is, I was estatic.

NaNoWriMo stands for "National Novel Writing Month", and is a competition based on the production of prose.

The thing is though, is that you have to write a 50,000 word story in a month. It's all about quantity, not quality, and that's what makes it fun. Even if you're some Joe Schmo that writes like a cat tied to a stick, you can compete with other artsy-fartsy posh-posh writers. I've always wanted to write a story, but I always quit after the eleventh page. Now that there's a competition where you can submit crap and still win, I'm interested.

Perhaps I'll take a stab at it.

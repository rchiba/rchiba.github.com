--- layout: post title: '' tags: - Blast From the Past status: private type: post published: false meta: blogger_blog: ryochiba.blogspot.com blogger_author: Ryohttp://www.blogger.com/profile/08925049298023370483noreply@blogger.com blogger_permalink: /2006/04/ive-spent-whole-day-trekking-my-way.html --- I've spent the whole day trekking my way through busy airport terminals and the like to eventually get where I am now, in my room. Sitting in my room, the peaceful headquarters for the operations that I so dearly treasure. Operations and tasks such as the kind I am attempting now, typing away. Rather, hacking away at the keyboard. My typing skills have slowed and my once nimble fingers have become clumsy due to lack of computer.

Salt Lake City and Moab were rather ace. Four days of hopping rocks in the desert has given me a rather distinguished tan, which I don't really like.

But, the disturbing things about the trip were not the events that surrounded it, but instead my thoughts and my mind during the trip. Desperate moral quandary, infatuation, a nagging feeling, a happy feeling, things that I cannot understand. Must be the whole "confused teenager" act that I'm supposed to follow. It makes me want to buy a few composition notebooks and write sad things about my life.

I've had these dreams during my vacation. Apparently, I cannot escape that which I try to escape.

School.

English class in particular. But, all throughout the last few nights, when I was supposed to be sleeping, relaxed, content, I was having nightmares about school. Absurd, really. In these dreams, I would encounter surprise exams about books I haven't read, be prompted to turn in assignments and projects that I had not heard about, forget my locker combination or room number, or receive a failing grade on a major test. In the aftermath of these nightmares, I would wake up in a dazed, cold-sweat trying to find the lost assignment or to remember what my project was.

Fear.

I try to get away, but my attempts are always futile. Spring break hasn't really given me a "break" so far.

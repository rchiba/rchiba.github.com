I wake up to a brightly lit hallway. Walls are beige, floors linoleum, air recycled. All around me are my friends. They talk and laugh, joke with each other. The hallways are crowded, I see no windows. I start to walk around, and as my vision comes to me, I see other hallways that branch out from the main hallway.

Looking down at the floor, I see hundreds of plastic shopping bags. I suppose they just appeared. Everyone in the hallway simultaneously picks them up. They mput them on their heads, seal the connection between the edge of the bag and their neck.

I panic.

Running, screaming, ripping bags off of heads, yelling at people I care about the most.

But when they notice me, they just laugh and joke around. Their faces show no sign of distress. Do they notice the bags? Mindless drivel comes out of their mouths, I don't understand, they appear happy.

My head spins, I'm hysterical, crying, confused. I start to shove people out of my way, moving my legs as fast as I can, trying to get out this massive group suicide / plastic bag hat celebration.

At the end of one of the hallways is a window. Outside is a senic view of wooded cliffs overlooking a sparkling ocean.

A man takes my hand and leads me to a chair.

"Do I have a disease where I lose my memory every day?"
"No."
"Then what is this?"
"You have a disease where you see words on things that don't exist."


I wake up.

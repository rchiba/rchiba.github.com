This data usually consists of latitude and longitude coordinates, though it can also include altitude and place names."

It's a fun way to enhance your photographic experience. Basically, you're adding extra data to your photographs so they can be traced to pinpoint locations on Earth. Plus, it's a few free downloads (and uploads) away.

First, download Picasa [LINK], and Google Earth [LINK] if you don't have these programs already. I use both programs often. Although, I like Google Earth better because I'm not used to Picasa's interface.


















After installation, Picasa will automatically grab photos from your hard drive and such, then display them in its fancy viewport.

Things are pretty self explanatory. Folders of photos on left, photos in the middle, double click a photo to edit, etc.





















Click on a photo you want to geotag, then hit Tools->Geotag->Geotag With Google Earth
Truly, convenience at its best.
Keep in mind that you can select more than one photo to geotag in bulk.






















Google Earth will then load up. Line the yellow crosshairs up with the place you took the photo. In my case, I'm using an old photo taken when i lived in Alabama. I'm placing the crosshairs where my old house is, off of Manningham Road in Greenville, Alabama. You can see that there is a highway behind my house. It lead to many sleepless nights.

When you're satisfied with your location, just hit "geotag", and the location is saved within your image file somewhere in its EXIF data.














After you've geotagged a bunch of photos, you can upload them to Panoramio [LINK], so other people can see your "map of photos". With Panoramio, you can upload and map your photos for free, then share your map with other people. The site automatically gets the location data Picasa saved onto the photos and places your photos on a map. It's neat. It's free. That's good.

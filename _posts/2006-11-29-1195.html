It's something worth complaining about, I guess.

Recently, I've been listening to harder rock (notice how I use harder) due to certain people. You know it's time to jump a station when you can hum chains of songs on the playlist. New music sounds fresh. Plus, Tif told me that I need to "feel the beat".

But, the thing is, it's not about feeling the beat. It's about my confidence.

In my mind, I'm often faced with the conflict of Arrogance and Confidence vs. Humbleness and Negative Self-worth. I dislike arrogant people, so I try to shy away from that as much as possible. Arrogance is something distasteful, vile, repulsive, yet so plentiful. But, sometimes it feels like you have to be arrogant to feel happy. I look at myself and see nothing of note, and that's good, or at least I used to feel as if it's supposed to be good.

It's not. It makes me weak, dark, and boring. Boring, boring, boring. Ordinary. A stupid potato. I sit around, average, ordinary, a simple creature. And the thing is, I can't call it "humble" anymore. It's more like "screwed up". Something is wrong.

Arrogant people are vibrant and loud, confident in their stride, simply exciting. They get what they want and they're admired.

I look at myself, and I can't help but try to shove the image away. Pathetic.

I look at myself, and I can't help feeling afraid. Afraid that I'll lose things. If I keep sitting in my chair feeling sorry for myself, I'll lose everything. Tif... friends... schoolwork... my will to exist... If I stop believing in myself, they'll stop believing in me. And then things end.

I'll lose them all because of my frail mindset. I feel pointless. And I know I'm making a mistake. Even if it means brainwashing myself into believing in myself, I know I need to change.

In the classroom, I watch students walk in and sit themselves down. I hunch over my books and peer from the corner of the classroom. All of them, they talk to each other powerfully, exchanging loud jokes, hand gestures. On their faces are these bold smiles. Smiles that don't die, can't die. They're indescribably content, they're so confident. I struggle to convince myself that I can pick myself up and save the future I don't believe in..

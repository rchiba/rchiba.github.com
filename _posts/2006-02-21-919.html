--- layout: post title: '' tags: - Blast From the Past status: private type: post published: false meta: blogger_blog: ryochiba.blogspot.com blogger_author: Ryohttp://www.blogger.com/profile/08925049298023370483noreply@blogger.com blogger_permalink: /2006/02/today-my-english-teacher-was-absent.html --- Today my English teacher was absent.

Thus, today's mood improved tenfold.

The rumor is that she had a mental breakdown at "the Academy" that she teaches at before she comes to my school. I heard from a friend that she supposedly cursed at her students and went bonkers.

I hope she doesn't come back.

But, I know she will.

Besides my English teacher's temporary demise, I've encountered a number of high-tension social situations today.

One such situation was when I was rude to someone before lunch. Due to my unacceptable order to leave me alone (which can be attributed to Health class, which I have right before lunch) caused a girl that I know to hate me for the day.

The following hours were full of heavy guilt and self-hate. That made my day worse by tenfold.

But, a few hours ago, I sent an apology email to her explaining my situation and pleading for forgiveness.

Luckily, I just recieved an email from her that stated her acceptance of my apology.

Such a happy ending to a rather odd situation. The whole scenario demonstrates the importance of proper apologies. If I hadn't taken the time to write her my apology email, she would still hate me right now. Because I had taken evasive action to avoid future conflicts (by apologizing), I have secured a relationship that may or may not positively affect my life in the future.

And now, I realize the importance of having other people "on your side" so to speak. No matter how weak, no matter how unimportant seeming, everyone is a contribution to the big pool of power that one must continuously harvest to survive in a cutthroat world. The philosophy is, the more people that you are on "good terms" with, the more people that can help you in a time of crisis. I find this concept facinating, as it seems to me like a sort of social insurance. All people should take great care in keeping relationships favorable. That's my two cents involving relationships, anyways. It's a rare thing to see me giving out relationship advice.

Another thing that I've realized...

Just like in books, people have distinct character roles that can either be flat or round. I know plenty of "shallow" people that seem rather lacking in depth, whether it be mental depth, emotional depth, social depth, or physical depth. Then again, I know quite a few people who are very "deep", so to speak. The people I attach onto so dearly and dependently are these people. Depth adds so much quality to one's overall human persona.

In middle school, depth was difficult to find. Now that I'm in high school, I know so many more complicated, sophisticated, intelligent, and deep people. I think middle school was a bad experience because of this fact.

Just something to be thankful about.

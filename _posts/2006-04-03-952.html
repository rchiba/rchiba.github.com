You never cease to amaze me.

I'm talking about Kim Komando, a technology writer that appears every week in the local newspaper. Today, she decided to answer questions about how to clear a web browser's cache, fix a virus, and/or what a "control panel" actually is. The horrible thing about it is that she's being doing this, answering exceedingly simple and similar questions, for the past few years. The fact that people still don't understand what the control panel is casts a shadow of doubt over the advancement of society.

The "Technology" page of the newspaper comes out Monday, and its always full of mindless, stale, idiotic tech news. Sometimes, actually most of the times, I feel like the editors of the newspapers and the wire writers meant the text to be read by monkeys. This Monday, one of the largest columns was about a new "Desperate Housewives" computer game. On the side of the paper, it mentions a webpage that lets one browse through the Bible, and in end different languages. Obviously for the monumental amounts of people who enjoy reading the Bible in ten languages.

And to add ugly to injury, Steve Jobs is present in a large image that is in the center of the whole web of monkey stool sample. How much longer will "we", as a society and species, have to cater to iPod-addicted, Steve-worshiping, Shallowtelevisionshow-watching, Bible-reading morons?

To tell you the truth, I'm beginning to think that it will be a long time before the Tech page in the newspaper cleans up, if ever.

Giving up on the newspaper, I've spent about 3 hours drawing something up. Tomorrow is a half day, which is a prime opportunity to knock out some more drawing while I'm at it. Hopefully, homework will be minimal over the course of the week. I'm just getting settled in.
